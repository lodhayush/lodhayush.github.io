New Delhi, India

I am a Master’s student in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at National Institute of Technology Delhi, working under the supervision of Dr. Nisha Singh Chauhan.

My research interests lie in computer vision, deep learning, and pattern recognition, with a particular focus on handwriting recognition and document understanding. My recent work includes:

  • Transformer-based handwriting recognition — a system that jointly leverages online (stroke) and offline (image) features, published at ACPR 2025.
  • NoTeS-Bank — a benchmark for neural transcription and search over handwritten scientific notes, evaluating vision-language models on evidence-based and open-domain VQA.
  • From Chunks to Graphs — a training-free multimodal retrieval framework that replaces chunk-based RAG with structure-aware subgraph retrieval over document graphs, developed during a research internship at the Computer Vision Center (UAB, Barcelona).

I have collaborated with the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Unit (CVPRU) at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, working with Prof. Umapada Pal’s group on handwriting recognition and document analysis, and contributed to the ICDAR 2025 Handwritten Notes Understanding Challenge.

  2. NoTeS-Bank: Benchmarking Neural Transcription and Search for Scientific Notes Understanding
    Aniket Pal, Sanket Biswas, Alloy Das, Ayush Lodh, Priyanka Banerjee, Soumitri Chattopadhyay, Dimosthenis Karatzas, Josep Lladós, and C. V. Jawahar
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2504.09249, 2025
    Abs arXiv Bib

    NoTeS-Bank is a benchmark for evaluating neural transcription and search on handwritten scientific notes containing equations and diagrams. It defines two tasks — evidence-based VQA with localization and open-domain VQA with domain classification — to assess vision-language fusion, retrieval, and multimodal reasoning.
